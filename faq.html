Frequently Asked Questions

PROOF exists to help more films get made — not replace filmmaking.

Traditional proof-of-concept filmmaking costs tens of thousands of dollars before a project ever gets traction. Many writers, directors, and emerging creators simply do not have access to those resources.

PROOF was built as a bridge between screenplay and screen — a way to help creators communicate tone, emotion, atmosphere, and cinematic vision without requiring full-scale production upfront.

Is PROOF trying to replace filmmakers, actors, or traditional production?

+

No. We're not replacing filmmakers, actors, or production — we respect that craft too much.

Our goal is simple: help more stories survive long enough to actually get made.

We help you show your project early, so it's easier to explain, build momentum, and bring people on board.

Why does PROOF exist?

+

Most scripts die in development because no one can picture them.

A real proof-of-concept short film costs $20,000–$50,000+. Most writers can't spend that just to show tone and potential.

PROOF exists to make that affordable — so you can show people what you already see in your head.

What problem does PROOF solve?

+

Scripts ask readers to imagine everything — tone, pacing, world. Most people can't do that from words alone.

We turn your idea into something people can actually watch, so they get it faster — and feel it.

Why not just pitch the script normally?

+

Because pitching is hard — even for great writers.

Plenty of people write brilliant scenes but freeze in a pitch meeting, or can't explain tone out loud.

PROOF gives you something to show instead of just something to say.

Is PROOF “AI-generated content”?

+

We use modern tools, but we're not selling you software output.

You're paying for storytelling judgment — the tone, pacing, and editing that make it actually work.

The tools help. The story leads.

Why not just use AI tools myself?

+

Anyone can generate images now. Making something that actually feels real is the hard part.

Most raw AI output breaks the illusion fast — inconsistent faces, flat emotion, disconnected pacing. Technically impressive, emotionally empty.

We combine the tools with real storytelling judgment — structure, continuity, pacing, atmosphere — so the result feels made, not generated.

You already see something in your head. We help other people finally see it too.
